all the updates regarding the Syria-Turkey earthquakes since February 6


February 20
- A 6.3-magnitude earthquake hits the town of Uzunbag in Turkey's Hatay Province. At least three people died and 213 were injured. There are still people trapped in the rubble, conditions are unknown.

February 20
- As of February 20, over 47,800 deaths have been confirmed - over 41,100 deaths in Turkey and over 6,600 in Syria.

February 19
- Death toll from the Syria-Turkey earthquakes passes 46,000. The United States sends out an additional $100 million.

February 9
- Charity appeals in the UK are launched for Turkey and Syria as death toll rises above 12,000. The UK sends out aid in forms of medical kits, tents, blankets as well as a team of search-and-rescue specialists.

February 8
- United States sends in support, joining rescue teams from Russia, China, India, Mexicon as well as the Middle East.

February 8
- Turkey reveals 6,597 people have died while 38,224 people are injured. The death toll soon approaches 10,000 - at least 6,957 in Turkey while more than 2,500 are dead in Syria.

February 7
- The United Nation's Undersecretary General for humanitarian Affairs, Martin Griffiths, releases $25 million from its Central Emergency Respond Fund for Turkey and Syria.

February 7
- Around 1 pm, Turkey's Vice President Fuat Oktay announces the death toll has hit 3,419 people. Syrian authorities report 1,602 deaths.

February 7
- Around 8 am, a 5.6-magnitude earthquake is reported. Irag sends out two humanitarian relief planes containing food and medical supplies.

February 7
- Around 1 am, Syria's volunteer civil defence organisation, White Helmets, report more than 2,800 deaths and hundreds of injuries and families trapped under the rubble in north-western Syria.

February 6
- Spain, Italy, Slovakia send out rescue teams to Turkey. Teams from Bulgairs, Croatia, France, Greece, Hungary, Malta, the Netherlands, Poland and Romania send out first responders.

February 6
- At 3 pm, the death toll in both Turkey and Syria surpasses 1,000 and then 2,300 around 6 pm. Lebanon sends out emergency service personnel to Turkey.

February 6
- Spain, Italy, Slovakia send out rescue teams to Turkey. Teams from Bulgairs, Croatia, France, Greece, Hungary, Malta, the Netherlands, Poland and Romania send out first responders.

February 6
- At 11 am, reports around the earthquake in Syria emerge. More than 300 people have died in northern Syria.

February 6
- Around 9 am, the death toll rises to 200 people. More and more countires are sending support.

February 6
- More than 2,100 aftershocks followed both earthquakes.

February 6
- Following the earthquake was a 6.7-magnitude earthquake around 4:28 am hitting the Kahramanmaras Province of Turkey.

February 6
- Around 4:17 am, a 7.8-magnitude earthquake hits southern and central Turkey, as well as northern and western Syria.
